Next Steps

What Should Happen Now

Three targeted actions — each tied to a specific finding, owner, and measurable expected outcome.

01

Peak-Hour Tip Nudge

Owner: Product · Rider Experience

A/B test pre-selected default tip amounts shown immediately at dropoff during peak commute hours (7–9 AM, 5–8 PM). Reduce friction in the tipping flow at the exact moment tip rates collapse.

Expected: +$1.6M driver earnings/mo at +2pp tip improvement across ~8M peak rides
02

Airport Fare Driver Incentive Audit

Owner: Driver Operations · Finance

Audit the airport fare split isolating base fare vs TLC-mandated surcharges. If a platform margin gap remains after removing the airport_fee, pilot a targeted driver airport bonus for experienced drivers.

Expected: −15% airport-decline rate among experienced drivers
03

Low-Value Ride Routing Pilot

Owner: Data Science · Marketplace Engineering

A/B test the 3-feature Random Forest as a real-time routing signal. When a low-value ride is predicted for a given driver, surface a higher-value nearby request instead before locking dispatch.

Expected: +5% avg revenue per driver-hour in test cohort
Methodology Notes

Assumptions & Limitations

Context needed before acting on these findings.

  • 10% sample: All percentages and ratios are representative of the full 20.9M rides. Absolute ride counts are estimates scaled ×10. Raw chart counts reflect the sample directly.
  • "Low-value" definition: Defined as the bottom 20% of total_fare — an arbitrary data-driven threshold. A business deployment would calibrate this against operational cost floors and driver hourly minimums.
  • Airport fee structure: The airport_fee ($2.50–$5.00) is a TLC-mandated surcharge included in total_fare but not discretionary platform margin. Part of the driver pay ratio gap may reflect this structure. A full audit isolating base fare vs surcharges is required before acting on Finding 2.
  • Tip rate causality: The data shows tip rate patterns by hour — not causes. Differences may reflect commuter demographics, business-account riders, or app UX defaults rather than driver behavior alone.
  • Model deployment: The Random Forest was trained on a 10% sample. Production use would require the full dataset, real-time surge signals, borough-level zone data, and A/B testing before rollout.
  • Single month: January 2026 data only. Seasonal effects (holiday travel, weather, tourism) may affect generalizability to other months.
